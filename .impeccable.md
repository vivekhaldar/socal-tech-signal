## Design Context

### Users

Technical founders, software and AI builders, engineering and security leaders, and hands-on operators deciding which in-person events across Orange County, Los Angeles, and San Diego are worth their time. They often scan quickly on a phone while planning the coming week.

### Brand Personality

Discerning, local, energetic. The voice is concise and editorial: confident enough to recommend, honest about caveats, and never promotional or breathless.

### Aesthetic Direction

An opinionated technical field guide: acid-lime signal fields, dense near-black type, hard cobalt and vermilion accents, geometric display typography, and abstract network-routing marks. It should feel closer to an experimental AI lab poster than a newspaper or corporate event directory. Light-first, flat-color, fast-loading, touch-friendly, and highly readable. Avoid ruled-paper backgrounds, serif-led editorial styling, cyan-on-dark AI clichés, glassmorphism, and generic card grids.

### Design Principles

1. Make the recommendation hierarchy obvious before presenting the complete list.
2. Optimize for one-handed mobile scanning without hiding any events.
3. Let each region have a recognizable visual signal while preserving one publication identity.
4. Keep editorial judgment visible through concise “why go” copy and candid access caveats.
5. Separate weekly content from the durable visual system so a new issue can be generated without redesigning the page.
