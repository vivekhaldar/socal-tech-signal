37 curated event entries. Southern California has a real builder week ahead. LA has the deepest technical slate, including Claude Code, defense cybersecurity, and a two-day spatial-AI hackathon. OC is strongest on practical learning—vibe coding, quantum computing, and AI operator workflows—while San Diego has an unusually good run of small, focused developer communities.

Three to circle.

The strongest reasons to rearrange the week.

Editor’s pick 01

AI Tinkerers Orange County ↗

Tuesday, July 21 · 6:00–8:00 p.m. · Mission Viejo · Approval required

The strongest OC builder room of the period: live systems, code and architecture internals, technical Q&A, and “demos, not decks” for practitioners building agents, autonomous workflows, and production LLM integrations. Capacity is limited to 50.

Editor’s pick 02

Worlds in Action Hack: SIGGRAPH Edition ↗

Saturday–Sunday, July 18–19 · 9:00 a.m.–5:00 p.m. · Los Angeles · Free application

The week’s strongest hands-on event: two days building world models, spatial AI, Gaussian-splat and WebXR systems, multi-agent environments, and creative-AI prototypes. It includes technical mentorship, API credits, and a $10,000 prize pool.

Editor’s pick 03

OpenAI Build Week Community Meetup — San Diego ↗

Tuesday, July 21 · 5:00–8:30 p.m. · San Diego · Approval required

A Codex-focused build session with demos, guided hands-on time, and $100 in Codex credits for checked-in attendees. The exact venue is released after approval.

A two-day public build sprint for turning an idea into an application or automation with AI and no-code tools. The program is broad enough for nontechnical founders, but the primary registration page was already showing a waitlist.

Orange County AI Office Hours ↗

Bring a laptop or a stuck project to this informal biweekly working session at HanaHaus. Best for people who want collaborator feedback or a low-pressure entry point, not a formal talk.

Tartini AI Leadership for the New Era — Part 1 ↗

The opening session in a three-part Ticket 500 series for entrepreneurs implementing AI. It starts with executive leadership: defining success, understanding the available tools, and preserving human intelligence in the operating model.

Orange County AI Vibe Coding ↗

A practical coworking meetup around Claude Code, Codex, and Gemini. The host explicitly welcomes beginners with laptops as well as experienced builders working on their own projects.

Wednesday, July 15 · 6:30–8:30 p.m. · Irvine · Free · Approval required

★ The Quantum World: Past, Present, and Future ↗

OC ACM’s technical overview of quantum platforms, error correction, software stacks, and emerging applications. Parking validation is provided.

Thursday, July 16 · 6:00–8:00 p.m. · Newport Beach · Free

Building Your Own AI Chief of Staff ↗

A small workshop promising to cover how professionals can build, train, and deploy an autonomous AI chief of staff. The listing is thin, so treat this as exploratory rather than a proven marquee program.

Thursday, July 16 · 6:00–8:30 p.m. · Irvine

Agentic AI for Small Business — OC Startup Roundtable ↗

A grounded operator discussion about where agents can help with marketing, sales, customer service, finance, and operations—and where they create risk or needless complexity. This is aimed at founders and small-business leaders rather than engineers.

Tuesday, July 21 · 6:00 p.m. · Newport Beach

Tartini AI Leadership for the New Era — Next Session ↗

The next Ticket 500 session continues the entrepreneur-focused AI leadership series. Attend the July 14 opening first if possible; the venue calendar lists another installment on July 27.

Tuesday, July 21 · 6:00–8:00 p.m. · Irvine

tech night! Orange County ↗

A developer-community social at University Town Center built around genuine peer connection, with an explicit no-recruiter and no-sales-pitch policy. Choose this over a generic mixer when your goal is meeting local software people rather than watching a formal program.

Tuesday, July 21 · 6:00–8:00 p.m. · Mission Viejo · Approval required

★ AI Tinkerers Orange County ↗

The strongest OC builder-room listing of the period: live implementation demos, code and architecture internals, no sales pitches, and room for technical breakouts.

★ Velocity: Cyberspace Warfare for Space Industry Engagement Day ↗

A focused government–industry forum on defensive cyber capabilities for space systems, with operational briefings, technical breakouts, and potential partnership paths.

Startup Cafe: Elevator Pitch Workshop ↗

An interactive founder workshop for drafting, testing, and tightening a short pitch for fundraising, recruiting, sales, media, and partnerships. It is operator-focused rather than AI-specific, but the agenda is concrete and practical.

Pacific Foundry: Space & Defense Intelligence Briefing ↗

A closed-door, 40-seat briefing for defense and dual-use operators, investors, and government leaders, followed by a larger mixer. High signal but not generally accessible: the organizer explicitly says there is no open application path without an invitation or sponsor referral.

AI Agents Happy Hour — South Bay ↗

No panel and no pitch night: builders, founders, and operators compare what is working and breaking in production agent systems. The listing had 85 attendees when checked, so register early.

The technical warm-up for Worlds in Action: researcher lightning talks, spatial-camera access, Gaussian-splat and world-building workshops, project pitches, and team formation. It is worthwhile even for builders who are still deciding whether to join the weekend hackathon.

Saturday–Sunday, July 18–19 · 9:00 a.m.–5:00 p.m. · Los Angeles · Free application

★ Worlds in Action Hack: SIGGRAPH Edition ↗

A high-signal build weekend for spatial AI, world models, 3D GenAI, multi-agent environments, and WebXR. Hacker, mentor, researcher, volunteer, and spectator applications are available.

Monday, July 20 · 6:00–8:00 p.m. · Santa Monica · Approval required

Broken Data Club LA with Matchbook AI ↗

A deliberately small room for data, analytics, governance, MDM, BI, and platform leaders dealing with the unreliable enterprise data underneath AI systems. It is explicitly neither a product demo nor a generic AI meetup.

Tuesday, July 21 · 5:30–7:30 p.m. · Venice

Agents & APIs Los Angeles Developer Meetup ↗

Postman, Vercel, and Render speakers will cover context-aware engineering agents, dependency-aware API changes, live agent-driven fixes and tests, and the evolution of production agent architectures.

Tuesday, July 21 · 5:30–8:30 p.m. · Culver City

The AI Shift in Cybersecurity ↗

ISSA-LA’s practitioner session on how AI is changing vulnerability management, offensive security, secure development, security operations, GRC, hiring, and organizational design. Dinner, drinks, and two CPE credits are included.
