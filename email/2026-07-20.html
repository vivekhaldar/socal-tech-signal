Three to circle.

The strongest reasons to rearrange the week.

Editor’s pick 01

FutureCon Orange County Cybersecurity Conference ↗

Thursday, July 23 · 8:00 a.m.–5:15 p.m. · Garden Grove · $200 full day / $50 afternoon · Approval required

The most substantial security program in the window combines a CISO panel with sessions on browser threats, machine-speed attacks, autonomous-agent attack surfaces, regulated-data access, and bot/API security. It is sponsor-heavy, but the agenda is unusually current and employed IT professionals can earn up to 10 CPE credits; students and most sales or business-development applicants are ineligible.

Editor’s pick 02

Secure AI-Assisted Development: Building Safer Code with Claude Code, Codex, and Security-Driven Prompts ↗

Wednesday, July 22 · 5:30–8:30 p.m. · Santa Monica · Free

OWASP Los Angeles is offering a practical, demo-driven session on secure prompting, code review, testing, and remediation with AI coding tools. This is the strongest broadly accessible LA event for developers who want concrete techniques rather than another high-level AI panel.

Editor’s pick 03

Future of Work: Social Impact Hackathon ↗

Saturday–Sunday, August 1–2 · 9:00 a.m.–5:30 p.m. · Rancho Bernardo · Free application

Nonprofit leaders, community builders, technologists, and social entrepreneurs will work alongside AI teammates and mentors on real outreach, grants, research, operations, and project-management needs. The participating organization stays in the driver’s seat, no technical background is required, and attendance is free but selected by application.
